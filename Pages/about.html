About Me



Finding Myself

My name is Carlos Rossi. I am a student at Broward College pursuing a degree in Information Technology as well as a minor in Mathematics. I was born in city of Barquisimeto, Venezuela. However, when I was thirteen, my father accepted a job in Weston, Florida, and my young family was quickly uprooted. In Weston, my father, mother, younger sister, and I quite literally found ourselves in a foreing land with a foreing language. Since I was enrolled in english lessons back home it only took me roughly a year to become fluent in english. We moved here around the start of summer vacation. So in those first couple of months before I met friends in school; I stayed home and tinkered with my computer. Eventually this would lead to a cycle of completely ruining the PC and then finding ways to fix it. It was around my final year of highschool that I began to acquire an interest in software and its development. I enrolled in AP Computer Science, my first programming class with Java as a language.

Making Progress

I have always been intrigued and captivated by problem solving; so, after graduating from high school, I chose to persue a career in software engineering. Additionally I made a personal goal to complete a minor in mathematics. As a kid I dreamed of one day building or designing something I could call my own, now as an adult I see that I will do that through the development of software. My interest in problem solving that eventually led me to programming also made me gravitate towards mathematics. I am excited and eager to utilize my passion and knowledge in a professional environment. Currently, I work as a mathematics tutor and as a full-time studen. When my tutor cap is on, I am responsible for assisting my student in understanding the material the view in class, devicing effective ways of testing those skills and helping them with homework. When I am a student, I have been able to maintain 3.0 GPA every semester; while I also enjoy getting involved in various on-campus organizations including the TALK CSE intership.

For The Future

While I am still working towards my Associates of Science degree, I am eager to gain experience as a engineer in a software development related discipline. I hope to be challenged in a way that will allow me to develop my leadership and technical skills as well as communication abilities. I feel that by gaining these skills, I will be better able to succeed as a leader in my future professional career. I hope to work in a fast-paced, high-energy work environment after graduation.
